逐玉 · 金句与感悟

Pursuit of Jade

quotes · notes · learnings · 金句与感悟

theme 01 · identity

jade isn't born, it's found in the rough

The whole title is a metaphor. 玉 (jade) in Chinese culture = virtue, moral worth, inner character. Fan Changyu literally has the character 玉 in her name — she is the jade. But nobody sees it at first because she's a butcher's daughter. The show's argument: dignity isn't assigned by birth, it's revealed by how you live.

「感谢你们有眼不识金镶玉。」
"Thank you for failing to recognize gold inlaid with jade."
— Xie Zheng / Yan Zheng
learning

Being rejected or overlooked by the wrong people isn't failure — it's freedom. The people who can't see your worth just saved you from giving them your time.

theme 02 · loyalty vs. justice

what happens when the throne betrays the people who died for it

The three military families — Xie (谢), Wei (魏祁林's original family), and the Qi imperial house — were all bound together by loyalty to the crown. But the late emperor Qi Yi used that loyalty against them, engineering the Jinzhou massacre to eliminate his own son, Crown Prince Chengde. Three families' fates destroyed — for an emperor's paranoia. Loyalty to a corrupt ruler isn't virtue. It's complicity.

「先帝要稳坐龙椅，李家要掌控朝堂，归根到底，他们都从未将最容易遭受战火牵连的百姓，和为国征战的将士们，放在心上。」
"The late emperor wanted to secure his throne, the Li clan wanted to control the court — in the end, neither of them ever truly thought about the civilians caught in the crossfire, or the soldiers who bled for this country."
— narrative analysis (Zhihu, 逐玉 thread)
learning

Loyalty without discernment is just obedience. The real question isn't "are you loyal?" — it's "loyal to what, and at whose expense?"

theme 03 · love

love in a market world

Changyu's declarations of love are never poetic — they're practical. She offers to slaughter pigs to support him. She says she'll "raise him." This is the show's radical move: making a woman's love loud, direct, and completely unashamed. Meanwhile Xie Zheng — the decorated war general — is the one undone by her.

「你若留下，我杀猪养你啊。」
"If you stay, I'll slaughter pigs and support you."
— Fan Changyu
「我算尽天下棋局，却算漏了自己的缘分。」
"I've calculated every game under heaven — but I forgot to account for my own fate."
— Xie Zheng
learning

The smartest person in the room can still be blindsided by feeling something real. Strategy doesn't inoculate you against love — it just makes you slower to admit it.

theme 04 · power & grief

the villain who was also a victim

Wei Yan (魏严) is the prime minister and apparent antagonist. But the finale reveals: he was manipulated by the late emperor through the woman he loved. He took the blame for the Jinzhou disaster he didn't fully cause. He spent 17 years as a power-hungry chancellor because grief and guilt curdled inside him. He's not excused — but he's explained. The real monster is the dead emperor no one can punish anymore.

theme

Power without accountability doesn't just corrupt the people who hold it — it corrupts the next generation who inherit the fallout. Qi Sheng became a puppet emperor. Qi Min became a tortured avenger. The late emperor's paranoia became everyone else's tragedy.

theme 05 · survival

just try to stay alive, one slap at a time

Fan Changyu's ethos isn't heroism — it's durability. She doesn't dream of glory; she dreams of 100 pigs. But her commitment to practical survival is what makes her actually dangerous in war. She's not fighting for ideology — she's fighting for the people she can name.

「世间万物，皆有其价。唯独真心，千金难换。」
"Everything in this world has a price. Only sincerity cannot be bought for a thousand gold."
— drama dialogue
「这辈子做个好猪，下辈子做个好人！」
"In this life, be a good pig. In the next, be a good person!"
— Fan Changyu
the core wound

三族皆忠，皆被辜负

Three families. All loyal to the crown. All betrayed by it. This is the central injustice the whole 40 episodes slowly unbury.

谢家 — the Xie family

Marquis Wu'an Xie Linshan was the military backbone of Great Yin. His reward? Framed for treason, slaughtered. His wife took her own life. His five-year-old son (Xie Zheng) was the only survivor, raised by the very uncle who may have helped engineer the cover-up.

loyal to country → killed by the emperor who sent them there
魏祁林 — Wei Qilin's line

Fan Changyu's father was originally Wei Qilin — a Wei family warrior, then a general under Xie Linshan. He was scapegoated for the Jinzhou disaster and had to change his name, live in hiding as a butcher in a small town, and eventually die with the secret locked in his chopping knife.

loyal to his lord → spent 17 years hiding from the people his lord served
齐家 / Crown Prince Chengde — the imperial casualty

Crown Prince Chengde was loyal to the kingdom — popular, militarily capable, beloved by the soldiers. His own father (Emperor Qi Yi) feared that popularity. So the emperor engineered the abandonment of the Jinzhou garrison while the prince was visiting the front — killing his own heir to protect his throne. The Xie and Wei families were just collateral damage to a royal patricide.

loyal to his father's empire → killed by his father
「那时的谢征，化名言正，入赘樊家，李怀安认出了谢征，却没有揭穿他的身份。」
"At that time, Xie Zheng — disguised as Yan Zheng — had married into the Fan family. Li Huai'an recognized him, but chose not to expose him."
— Zhihu analysis of the Li family thread
the learning

loyalty to a system ≠ loyalty to what's right

All three families were loyal — and all three were destroyed by the institution they served. The drama isn't saying loyalty is bad. It's saying blind loyalty to a corrupt ruler is indistinguishable from complicity. The people who survived and found meaning were the ones who eventually redirected their loyalty: to each other, to justice, to the truth — not to the throne.

「只要我活着，你就别想替旁人生儿育女。」
"As long as I live, don't even think about having children with someone else."
— Xie Zheng (intense loyalty redirected: to her, not to the crown)
ep 1–2 the snow and the soldier

Changyu finds a dying man in a snowstorm — a stranger who calls himself Yan Zheng. She doesn't know he's the Marquis Wu'an, the most feared general in Great Yin. She takes him in because she recognizes grief. This is the show's thesis: people who've lost something recognize it in others, even when disguised.

「他是我在雪地里捡到的一块上好羊脂白玉，美、润，也值钱，但他不属于我，迟早是要还的。」
"He's a piece of fine white jade I found in the snow — beautiful, smooth, and valuable. But he doesn't belong to me. Sooner or later, he'll have to be returned."
— Fan Changyu's inner monologue
learning

She already knows the love is temporary — and loves anyway. That's not delusion. That's courage.

ep 3–5 the contract marriage

They strike a deal: he pretends to be her husband to protect the family, she nurses him back to health. Classic fake-to-real setup, but the drama earns it because Changyu is completely clear-eyed the entire time. She negotiates the terms like a business arrangement — and that practicality is exactly what cracks him open.

「长玉，我娶你好不好？你不说话，我就当你答应了。」
"Changyu, will you marry me? If you don't say anything, I'll take that as a yes."
— Xie Zheng / Yan Zheng
「你今天吃陈皮糖了吗？那你现在想吃吗？」
"Did you eat any tangerine peel candy today? Do you want some now?"
— Xie Zheng (the iconic flirt line)
ep 6–15 lin'an arc — the best episodes

Everyone agrees: the Lin'an small-town arc is the show's peak. Slow build, warm market life, neighbors, pigs, case-solving, growing feelings — all while Xie Zheng hides who he is. The appeal isn't the romance alone, it's watching a war general discover what it feels like to be cared for as just a person. He's used to being feared. She just feeds him and argues with him.

「想要一家人整整齐齐，平平安安。」
"I just want the family to be whole, and safe."
— Fan Changyu (her only dream, stated simply)
learning

The most dangerous thing you can give someone who's only known duty and revenge is a glimpse of ordinary warmth. Xie Zheng was done from episode 8.

ep 16–25 war, separation, and identity reveal

Xie Zheng is pulled back to his real identity. He's Marquis Wu'an again. The distance between them becomes class, title, rank. Changyu refuses to accept that — follows him to the battlefield and replaces him in a duel to protect his cover, killing the enemy general herself. The butcher's knife meets the battlefield.

「我不怕死，只怕我死了，没人护你。」
"I'm not afraid of dying — I'm afraid that if I die, there'll be no one left to protect you."
— Fan Changyu
「别躲我，我只有你。」
"Don't avoid me. You are all I have."
— Xie Zheng (the line that broke the internet)
ep 26–35 the truth about jinzhou / 锦州血案

The Jinzhou massacre is slowly unpacked. The truth: the late emperor orchestrated the garrison's destruction to eliminate Crown Prince Chengde. The Xie family, Wei Qilin, the soldiers — all collateral damage. Changyu discovers she's Wei Qilin's granddaughter. Xie Zheng discovers his uncle Wei Yan isn't purely the villain. Everyone is downstream of one dead emperor's paranoia.

「先帝忌惮承德太子威望日盛，于是痛下杀手，设局利用魏严对淑妃戚容音的爱意，把他引回京城。」
"The late emperor, threatened by Crown Prince Chengde's growing prestige, made his move — using Wei Yan's love for Consort Chi Rongyin as a trap to lure him back to the capital."
— plot summary (Tencent news, 逐玉 ep 40 recap)
learning

The person you've been blaming for 17 years might also have been manipulated. That doesn't erase the damage — but it changes what justice actually looks like.

ep 36–40 大结局 — the ending

Changyu becomes General (怀化大将军). Xie Zheng becomes Prince Regent — briefly — then steps back. A child emperor (Bao'er / Yu Qi, who is actually Crown Prince Chengde's biological grandson) takes the throne. The puppet emperor Qi Sheng dies by poison — quietly, alone, unrecorded. The real winners are people who never wanted power. And Changyu, who wanted 100 pigs.

「樊氏长玉，乃吾终身之侣，绝不相负，至死不渝。」
"Fan Changyu — she is the companion of my lifetime. I will never betray her. Until death, unchanging."
— Xie Zheng (final vow)
「齐昇独坐空殿，手抖接杯，未发一言，无声毙命。」
"Qi Sheng sat alone in the empty palace. Hands trembling, he accepted the cup — said nothing, died in silence."
— recap (360 Entertainment)
learning

A puppet emperor who reigned 17 years — no son, no ally, no obituary. Power without agency is just a cage with a crown on it.
